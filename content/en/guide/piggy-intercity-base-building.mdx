<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step: Constructing Your First Shelter">

Building a base in Piggy Intercity is a multi-stage process that requires careful planning. You cannot simply place walls anywhere; the game utilizes a grid-based system tied to specific "Claim Stakes." Following a structured approach ensures you don't waste precious materials on a poorly defended or inefficient layout.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate an Available Plot">
    Travel through Evalia to find a vacant plot. Look for the white boundary lines or a physical stake in the ground. If a plot is already claimed by another player, you will need to find a different server or a more remote location.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Claim the Stake">
    Interact with the plot stake. You may need a small amount of currency or a basic tool to finalize the claim. Once claimed, this area is yours to build on, and other players cannot place structures within your boundaries.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Set Up a Crafting Table">
    Before placing walls, you need a Crafting Table. This is the heart of **piggy intercity base building**. It allows you to convert raw logs into planks and scrap metal into reinforced plates.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Establish the Perimeter">
    Start with a foundation and basic walls. Focus on creating a single, secure room first. This room should house your bed (for respawning) and your storage crates to protect your most valuable loot.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Fortify and Expand">
    Once the basics are set, add windows for visibility and reinforced doors. As you level up your attributes in the full version, you will unlock larger blueprints and more durable materials.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Base Defense and Survival Strategy">

A base is only useful if it can keep the infected out. **Piggy intercity base building** includes a variety of defensive options, from simple barricades to complex traps. Because Evalia is an open world, threats can come from any direction, especially at night when infected activity increases.

When designing your layout, consider the "Air-Lock" strategy. By building two sets of doors at your entrance, you prevent infected (or hostile players) from rushing into your main storage area if you are followed home.
