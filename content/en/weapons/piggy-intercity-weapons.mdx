<Section icon={Shield} title="Combat Fundamentals in Evalia">

Surviving the open world of Evalia requires more than just running; it demands a deep understanding of the **piggy intercity weapons** system. Unlike the original Piggy escape maps, Intercity introduces a choice-driven RPG framework where combat has consequences. Every encounter with the infected or hostile NPCs tests your equipment durability, your resource management, and your tactical positioning.

The combat loop is built around three pillars: detection, engagement, and recovery. Players must balance their hunger and thirst levels, as low survival stats can negatively impact combat performance, such as reduced stamina for swinging melee weapons or slower movement speeds during a firefight.

| Mechanic | Impact on Combat | Strategy Recommendation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blocking** | Reduces incoming melee damage | Time blocks just before an enemy strike |
| **Stamina** | Limits melee swings and sprinting | Avoid spamming attacks; keep a reserve for escapes |
| **Headshots** | Massive damage bonus for ranged | Aim slightly above center mass for infected |
| **Durability** | Weapons may degrade or require maintenance | Always carry a backup melee tool |
| **Stealth** | Avoids unnecessary combat encounters | Crouch to reduce noise when near infected groups |

<Callout type="warning" title="Stamina Management">
Do not deplete your stamina bar entirely during a fight. If you reach zero stamina, you will be unable to block or sprint, leaving you completely vulnerable to the infected.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Melee Weapon Classification and Tier List">

Melee weapons are the backbone of any survivor's loadout in Piggy: Intercity. Because ammo is a finite and rare resource, you will spend the majority of your time defending yourself with blunt or bladed instruments. These weapons do not require ammunition but rely heavily on the player's stamina bar and timing.

In the current meta, melee weapons are ranked based on their swing speed, reach (hitbox size), and the amount of stagger they apply to enemies. A weapon with high stagger can interrupt an infected's attack animation, providing a vital window for a follow-up strike or a tactical retreat.

<Section icon={Target} title="Ranged Weapons and Ammo Tactics">

Ranged **piggy intercity weapons** provide the ultimate advantage: the ability to neutralize threats before they reach you. However, this power comes with the burden of ammo scarcity. Finding bullets in Evalia is difficult, often requiring players to explore dangerous "Infection Danger Routes" or spend hard-earned currency at shops.

Ranged combat is not just about clicking on enemies; it involves managing reload times and understanding the effective range of your firearm. A pistol might be effective at mid-range, but its damage drop-off makes it poor for sniping, whereas a rifle requires steady aim but can clear a path from a safe distance.

| Ammo Type | Compatible Weapons | Rarity | Typical Location |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Small Caliber** | Pistols, SMGs | Common | Residential buildings, small shops |
| **Shells** | Shotguns | Uncommon | Police stations, fortified bases |
| **Large Caliber** | Rifles, Snipers | Rare | Military outposts, high-tier chests |
| **Specialty** | Crossbows, Launchers | Very Rare | Quest rewards, secret locations |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Secure a Firearm">
    Check police stations or military checkpoints early in your run. These are high-risk areas but are the most likely to spawn a basic pistol or shotgun.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Conserve Your Bullets">
    Only use ranged weapons against "Tank" infected or when you are outnumbered. Using a bullet on a single, weak infected is a waste of resources.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Master the Reload">
    Reloading takes time and often slows your movement. Always reload *before* entering a new building or starting a quest interaction.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Aim for the Head">
    Headshots in Piggy: Intercity deal significantly more damage. This is essential for conserving ammo, as one headshot often equals three body shots.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Ammo Scavenging Tip">
Check under furniture and inside crates in "Safe Zones." Sometimes developers hide small ammo stashes in places players usually overlook while rushing to NPCs.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Combat Stats and Leveling Attributes">

The full version of Piggy: Intercity features an expanded attribute system that directly influences how you use your weapons. As you level up, you can invest points into different stats that change your combat effectiveness. Focusing on Strength will increase your melee damage and stagger potential, while Agility might improve your reload speed and stamina regeneration. Understanding these synergies is key to late-game survival in the more difficult regions of Evalia.

| Attribute | Effect on Weapons | Playstyle Synergy |

| **Endurance** | Increases total stamina and block duration | Defensive / Survivalist build |
